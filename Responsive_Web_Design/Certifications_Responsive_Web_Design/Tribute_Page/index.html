Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

  • 1756: Born on January 27 in Salzburg, Austria.
  • 1761: Begins musical education under his father, Leopold Mozart.
  • 1762: Embarks on first concert tour of Europe with his sister, Nannerl.
  • 1769: Composes his first symphonies and operas.
  • 1777: Leaves his position as a court musician in Salzburg, seeking independence in Vienna.
  • 1782: Marries Constanze Weber.
  • 1784: Produces a series of masterpieces including the symphonies "Jupiter" and "Prague".
  • 1786: Premieres his opera "The Marriage of Figaro".
  • 1787: Visits Prague where he is hailed as a composer.
  • 1788: Begins experiencing financial difficulties.
  • 1789: Composes the opera "Cosi fan tutte".
  • 1791: Composes his last opera "The Magic Flute" and his Requiem.
  • 1791: Passes away on December 5 in Vienna at the age of 35.
